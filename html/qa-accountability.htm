QA Accountability
work-style.htm ⮐

If a QA didn't test a critical path, or let a known issue slide
without properly communicating risk, that's on QA. There have
been times where I've felt I should have caught something, that I
overlooked, but I've been lucky not to work in places where QA
gets blamed for everything.

I can only remember one instance where someone said "QA really
messed up this time!", but that was quickly corrected when I showed
the Jira ticket where QA clearly flagged the risks. The team just
rushed to release and ignored the warnings.

I've read through QA forums and noticed on some teams QA gets
scapegoated because it's easier than fixing real process or ownership
issues. For example, when a feature is rushed out without clear
requirements, proper design review, or solid, well-tested code, bugs
are inevitable, but QA takes the blame for missing them. Sometimes
aggressive deadlines push teams to cut corners, causing defects
yet QA is held responsible rather than addressing the root cause
in planning or culture.

Like I said, I haven't really experienced this, but I've seen enough
discussions and heard from others that it's a real problem in some
places.
